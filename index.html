DeepCybo Prime on the cover of J. Phys. Chem. Lett.

Why it matters

This moment shows that the visual identity of the robot held up not only in product renders and exhibition settings, but also as part of a polished scientific publication image. It is a strong signal of the project's communicative quality and cultural reach.

Project 02

Noetix robotics product family / 松延动力

At Noetix Robotics, I worked on the upper-body industrial design language shared across multiple bionic and interactive robot products. This section includes Xiao Nuo as an interactive humanoid communication product and Hobbs 3.0 as a producible bionic service robot, both carrying forward the torso and neck design system I developed.

Scope Upper-body product identity, torso and neck surfacing, screen integration
Timeline Feb. 2025 - Apr. 2025

06

Xiao Nuo, interactive humanoid communication product

Xiao Nuo is not just a form study. It is a real interactive product used for face-to-face communication, public demonstration, and emotionally expressive interaction. These images combine surfaced CAD, prototype photography, and public event scenes to show how the upper-body design supports friendliness, readability, and presence.

Design focus

The key challenge in Xiao Nuo was balancing product intimacy with robotic credibility. The head, neck, chest screen, and shoulder transitions all had to work together so the robot could feel approachable while still reading as a coherent technology product.

07

Hobbs 3.0, producible bionic service robot

Hobbs 3.0 extends the same torso and neck design language into another product line. As you described it, Noetix Robotics Hobbs 3.0 is the world's first producible bionic service robot, featuring built-in cameras for eyes. It was displayed at the Appliance and Electronics World Expo in Shanghai, China.

Shared design language

Even though Hobbs 3.0 is not Xiao Nuo, it still carries the torso and neck system I designed. Including it here makes the Noetix section more accurate: it shows how one upper-body design language can scale across multiple interactive robot products with different faces, scenarios, and public roles.
